All Australians and international tourists have a role to keep out exotic pests and diseases. Xylella is present in a number of countries. Australia remains free of this exotic pest. We need your help to keep it this way.Check what you can and cannot bring into Australia, whether you are a:There is no evidence of xylella being successfully eradicated once it has established. For this reason, preventing the entry of this pathogen into Australia is vital.Some items, by law, are subject to certain import conditions to be allowed into Australia. secure any suspect specimens

Containment is critical. This could be as easy as bagging a suspect plant specimen that has symptoms or stopping an insect escaping by closing the doors on a shipping container.Controlling vectors and removing infected plant material are the only control options, in conjunction with movement restrictions on infected plant material, to limit its spread. Identification and use of resistant plant stock may provide a long term management option, as the disease would be nearly impossible to eradicate.
